Why I Built This

I'm Dan Woodruff, an independent consultant. After years of saving research links, client sites, tool references, and "read later" articles, I ended up with over 800 bookmarks in Chrome. Maybe 40% of them were actually useful. The rest were dead pages, sites that had moved, articles behind new paywalls, or links I'd saved during a project that ended years ago.

"I kept meaning to clean them up manually. But clicking through hundreds of links one at a time is exactly the kind of task that never makes it onto the to-do list."

I looked for a free tool that would do this automatically inside Chrome. The options I found either required creating an account, synced my data to an external server, or cost money for basic features. None of that felt right for a utility this simple.

So I built Bookmark Gardening Manager. It runs completely inside your browser, costs nothing, and does the one thing bookmark managers should do: help you keep only what's actually useful.

— Dan Woodruff, Dan Woodruff Consulting, LLC

Accuracy of Link Checking

The dead-link scanner checks URLs at the time of the scan. A link flagged as dead may be temporarily unreachable due to server downtime, firewalls, login requirements, or network conditions — it does not necessarily mean the page is permanently gone. Conversely, some broken links may not be detected if the destination server returns a non-standard response. Always review flagged results before deleting.
